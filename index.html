Prompt-to-Edit: Automated TTS Editing through Voice Prompts

Text-to-speech (TTS) systems have seen significant advancements in controllability, enabling users to manipulate prosody, emotion, accent, etc. However, pronunciation control, a crucial aspect of synthesized speech, often remains challenging. Conventional methods rely on phoneme sequence manipulation, which can be cumbersome and errorprone. We propose a prompt-based approach that allows users to edit the pronun- ciation of a word by simply providing the desired pronunciation as a speech prompt. Our approach builds upon a modular TTS architecture leveraging a self-supervised speech representation. Specifically, we embed the speech prompt and the text into a shared representation and automatically edit the intermediate representation of the TTS. Experimental results validate our method’s effectiveness in various settings, outperforming traditional baselines

"-std" refers to the approach using the rule based phoneme sequence, "-alt" refers to the approach using the best possible phoneme sequence.
